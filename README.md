# Adventure Works Global Sales & Operations Dashboard

This Power BI project provides a comprehensive analysis of Adventure Works’ global sales performance, profit margins, and supply chain efficiency. It combines executive-level KPIs, product profitability insights, and shipment/inventory trends to enable data-driven business decisions.

## Executive Overview
- Total Sales: $110M | Profit: $13M | Profit Margin: 11%  
- Visualizes yearly trends (2010–2014) across sales, cost, and profit.  
- United States leads in revenue ($54M reseller sales), while Australia shows strong online sales.  
- Highlights Bikes as the top-selling category with highest order volume.

![Executive Overview](Executive%20Overview.png)

## Profit Diagnostics
- Total Units Sold: 60K | Avg Unit Price: $1.8K | Profit per Unit: $207  
- Bikes contribute 83% of total profit, dominating overall sales.  
- Australia has the highest profit margin (34%), followed by Germany (22%).  
- Monthly tracking of unit price, cost, and margin % reveals strong mid-year profitability.

![Profit Diagnostics](Profit%20Diagnostics.png)

## Shipment and Inventory Analysis
- Total Freight Cost: $2.7M | Freight % of Sales: 2.5% | Avg Freight per Order: $87  
- North America incurs the highest freight costs.  
- June shows peak shipment activity for Bikes, with clear cost-to-balance correlation.  
- Provides a relationship view between inventory value and shipment cost, supporting optimization in logistics.

![Shipment and Inventory Analysis](Shipment,Inventory%20Analysis.png)

## Key Insights
- Bikes drive majority of sales and profit; improving logistics here yields major ROI.  
- Australia shows best efficiency with a high profit margin and balanced sales cost.  
- Freight costs remain steady but can be optimized in North America.  
- Clear link identified between inventory management and shipment expenses.
